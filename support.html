Getting Started

  1. Launch Tapsworth from your Applications folder
  2. Grant Accessibility permission when prompted (System Settings > Privacy & Security > Accessibility)
  3. Toggle on from the main window or the menu bar icon
  4. Choose your switch type from the dropdown — pick from 13 mechanical keyboard sounds

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I don't hear any sound when typing.

Make sure Tapsworth is toggled to "Active" and that you've granted Accessibility permission in System Settings > Privacy & Security > Accessibility. Also check that your Mac's volume is turned up.

Q: How do I grant Accessibility permission?

Open System Settings > Privacy & Security > Accessibility, then toggle Tapsworth on. You may need to click the lock icon first.

Q: Does Tapsworth record my keystrokes?

No. Tapsworth only detects that a key was pressed to play a sound. It never records, stores, or transmits what you type. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Q: How do I change the keyboard sound?

Use the "Switch Type" dropdown in the main window, or click the Tapsworth menu bar icon and select a different switch type.

Q: Can I use Tapsworth from the menu bar only?

Yes. Tapsworth lives in your menu bar where you can toggle it on/off, change switch types, and quit — all without opening the main window.

Q: Does Tapsworth work with external keyboards?

Yes. Tapsworth detects all key presses system-wide regardless of which keyboard you use.

Q: How do I quit Tapsworth?

Click the menu bar icon and select "Quit Tapsworth", or use Cmd+Q from the main window.

System Requirements

  • macOS 13.0 (Ventura) or later
  • Accessibility permission required
